This binary approach creates risk for everyone involved.

I'm proposing something different. By maintaining my current full-time position while building AI Lab @Mindgrub, I'm personally absorbing the transition risk. This approach transforms what would typically be a high-risk hiring decision into a measured, strategic partnership where success is demonstrated, not promised.

The only question remaining is how.

This creates two possible paths forward. Mindgrub can approach this as a traditional development project - hiring contractors, managing multiple vendors, navigating technical decisions, and hoping the pieces eventually come together. This path typically takes 6-12 months, costs $500K-$1M in development alone, and carries significant risk of failure or mediocrity.

Or Mindgrub can recognize the opportunity in front of them.
